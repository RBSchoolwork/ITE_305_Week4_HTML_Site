United States National Parks

The National Parks of the United States make for great adventures for sight-seers, hikers, and nature enthusiasts.

From paved trails to backcountry hikes, there is something for everyone to explore and experience in the national parks.

The wide variety of landscapes across the country allows visitors to experience the diverse natural beauty and cultural heritage of the United States.

History

The National Park Service (NPS) is a federal agency that manages and preserves the natural and cultural resources of the United States. The NPS was established in 1916 by President Woodrow Wilson and is responsible for overseeing more than 400 sites, including national parks, monuments, historic sites, and recreational areas. The NPS aims to protect and conserve these areas for the enjoyment of current and future generations.

Varying Landscapes

America’s national parks encompass an extraordinary range of landscapes, from deserts and mountains to forests, coastlines, and underground wonders. Mammoth Cave National Park protects the world’s longest known cave system, revealing a vast subterranean landscape shaped by winding passages and dramatic rock formations. Olympic National Park offers striking contrasts above ground, bringing together glacier-capped peaks, lush temperate rainforests, and rugged Pacific coastline within one remarkable park.

Add a visit to a National Park to your next vacation, you won’t regret it!
