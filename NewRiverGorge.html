New River Gorge National Park, located in West Virginia, makes for a fantastic day trip for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The New River was named as such because it was not on any maps at the time of its discovery, and the name stuck. Research into the river revealed that it is actually one of the oldest rivers in North America. Since its discovery, the New River has contributed to the local economy through coal mining, logging, and, in the modern era, recreation.

New River Gorge Bridge

The New River Gorge Bridge is an iconic landmark that spans the New River, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding canyon and forests. This steel arch bridge serves as a gateway to the New River Gorge National Park, where visitors can enjoy various recreational activities and wildlife viewing opportunities. The Fayette Station Road tour travels below the bridge and offers several trails for unique views of both the bridge and the gorge.

Kaymoor Trail

The Wolf Creek Trailhead provides access to the Kaymoor Trail, a scenic hiking route that leads to the historic Kaymoor Mine and offers stunning views of the New River Gorge. Hikers can enjoy the natural beauty of the area, including waterfalls, rock formations, and diverse plant life. This trailhead is located on one of the hairpin turns on Fayette Station Road, and has limited parking available.
